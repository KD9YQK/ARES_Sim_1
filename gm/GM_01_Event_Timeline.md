# GM Event Timeline

## Scenario

**Urban Search and Rescue — Apartment Collapse After Severe Storm**

One-line story:

A severe storm caused partial collapse of an older apartment building, scattered utility hazards, blocked access routes, and conflicting reports about trapped residents. The radio net’s job is to build the situation picture, route requests, and support simulated response coordination.

## Timing Rules

The active simulation is one hour.

Required NCO anchors:

- 00:00 — Net start and initial check-in.
- 00:15 — Simulation net status broadcast.
- 00:30 — Status roll call and location update.
- 00:45 — Simulation net status broadcast.
- 00:59 — Final roll call and net close.

Operator events should occur near the listed time, but the GM may slide them by a few minutes to maintain flow.

The GM should not force a perfect outcome. Allow operators and Net Control to interpret, clarify, miss details, recover, and adapt.

## Operator Roster

- OP 1 — Rescue Alpha
- OP 2 — Rescue Bravo
- OP 3 — Medical Staging
- OP 4 — Utilities / Damage Assessment
- OP 5 — Shelter / Resident Accountability
- OP 6 — Logistics / Access

## Chronological Timeline

### 00:00 — Net Start and Initial Check-In

NCO opens the net and requests each station to check in with callsign, tactical assignment, location, and immediate traffic.

Each operator has an initial check-in scene.

Expected duration: 6 to 12 minutes depending on operator count and NCO pace.

GM observes:

- Did operators use callsigns and tactical assignments?
- Did operators distinguish enroute from on scene?
- Did NCO capture locations?
- Did ANCO build the initial status board?
- Did anyone invent information not on their card?

No GM inject is required unless the net stalls or misses a major safety issue.

### 00:10 — OP 2 Event: East Access Blocked

Operator: Rescue Bravo

Scenario event:

Rescue Bravo arrives near the east access road. A large fallen tree and storm debris block vehicle access. A narrow sidewalk path may be passable on foot. No injured people are visible.

Purpose:

Tests whether the operator reports access limitations and whether NCO asks for useful clarification.

GM notes:

If NCO does not report road blockage to GM, leave the access issue partially unresolved.

### 00:15 — Required NCO Simulation Net Status Broadcast

NCO should broadcast that the simulation net remains active and directed.

GM observes:

- Did NCO remember the scheduled broadcast?
- Did NCO use callsign?
- Did NCO maintain directed-net control?

No operator event is scheduled here.

### 00:17 — OP 1 Event: Civilians Near Damaged Entrance

Operator: Rescue Alpha

Scenario event:

Rescue Alpha sees approximately 20 residents near the south entrance. Two have minor injuries. Rescue Alpha cannot confirm structural safety. Alpha Team Leader requests medical assistance through Net Control.

Purpose:

Tests priority traffic, resource requests, uncertainty, and safety communication.

GM notes:

If NCO requests guidance on medical assistance to GM, tell NCO that Medical can prepare a small triage element but will need safe route, patient count, injury type, contact point, and safety confirmation before movement.

### 00:20 — OP 4 Event: Downed Utility Line

Operator: Utilities / Damage Assessment

Scenario event:

Utilities finds a wire down near Oak and 6th, near standing water. Energized status is unknown. Pedestrians could enter the area.

Purpose:

Tests hazard reporting and whether NCO broadcasts safety-critical information to all stations.

GM notes:

If NCO fails to broadcast the hazard, allow future routing decisions to be more difficult.

### 00:24 — Conditional GM Inject: Medical Routing

Use this moment to respond to how NCO handled the 00:17 Rescue Alpha event.

If NCO reported the civilian/medical issue well:

Inject: Medical can send a small triage element, but will need a safe route and exact contact point later on.

Purpose:

Tests whether Net Control coordinates support rather than assuming field teams solved the issue.

### 00:30 — Required NCO Status Roll Call and Location Update

NCO should conduct a scheduled roll call and request location/status updates and priority traffic only.

Purpose:

Tests net discipline, logging, and situation-picture maintenance.

GM observes:

- Did NCO call stations in order?
- Did NCO ask for concise location/status updates?
- Did ANCO update team locations?
- Did NCO distinguish priority traffic from routine information?

### 00:30 — OP 5 Event During Roll Call: Conflicting Missing-Resident Report

Operator: Shelter / Resident Accountability

Scenario event:

During the roll call window, eight displaced residents arrive at the shelter. One resident says an elderly neighbor from apartment 3B may still be inside. Another resident thinks that same person left earlier. Information is conflicting and unconfirmed.

Purpose:

Tests whether an operator breaks appropriately with priority traffic and whether NCO handles uncertainty.

GM notes:

If NCO asks whether confirmed, OP 5 should say no.

If NCO treats the report as confirmed, note for debrief.

### 00:38 — OP 3 Event: Medical Needs Information Before Movement

Operator: Medical Staging

Scenario event:

Medical Team Leader is ready to send a small triage element if directed but needs safe route, patient count, injury type, contact point, and safety confirmation.

Purpose:

Tests whether support teams request needed information before movement and whether NCO coordinates rather than freelancing.

GM notes:

If NCO provides a route avoiding Oak and 6th and a contact point, Medical may later be reported as moving or arriving.

If route is unclear, Medical should hold.

If NCO sends Medical through Oak and 6th, treat it as a safety/routing error and require reroute.

### 00:42 — OP 2 Event: Voices on East Side

Operator: Rescue Bravo

Scenario event:

Rescue Bravo hears voices from above near the east side. They see damaged exterior stairs and broken second-floor windows. Number of people and injury status are unknown. They cannot safely reach them.

Purpose:

Tests incomplete information reporting and NCO clarifying questions.

GM notes:

If NCO asks good follow-up questions, OP 2 can determine there appear to be two people on or near an east-side balcony with no obvious injuries.

If NCO does not ask, leave the report vague and unresolved.

### 00:45 — Required NCO Simulation Net Status Broadcast

NCO should broadcast current net status and summarize major known items.

The summary should reflect only what the net actually knows.

Possible known items:

- East vehicle access blocked.
- Oak and 6th utility hazard.
- Civilians near south entrance.
- Medical staging or movement.
- Unconfirmed apartment 3B report.
- Possible people on east side or balcony.

Purpose:

Tests whether NCO can produce an accurate situation summary.

### 00:51 — OP 6 Event: Limited Logistics Resource

Operator: Logistics / Access

Scenario event:

Tree/debris removal will take longer than the remaining exercise time.

Purpose:

Tests NCO resource prioritization.

GM notes:

If NCO hesitates or never assigns, the resource remains staged and unused.

### 00:55 — OP 5 Event: Shelter Accountability Update

Operator: Shelter / Resident Accountability

Scenario event:

Shelter now has 17 residents. A newly arrived resident says the apartment 3B occupant left with family before the collapse.

Purpose:

Tests correction of earlier information and handling of changing reports.

GM notes:

None

### 00:57 — GM Final Stabilization Inject

GM provides a final status through NCO/ANCO based on actual net performance.

Choose one:

Good coordination ending:

Incident is stabilizing. Medical reached the south contact point. Utility hazard is marked. Shelter accountability is improving. East-side residents are identified and awaiting technical rescue.

Mixed coordination ending:

Incident remains active. Medical movement was delayed. Utility hazard is known but not fully controlled. Shelter accountability is incomplete. East-side voices or balcony residents remain unresolved or poorly documented.

Poor coordination ending:

Situation picture is fragmented. Several hazards are known but not routed clearly. Medical and logistics support were delayed or misdirected. Final roll call should focus on reconstructing current status.

### 00:59 — Required NCO Final Roll Call and Net Close

NCO conducts final roll call.

Stations should report final location, status, and unresolved priority traffic.

GM observes:

- Did NCO know where each team was?
- Did unresolved items get identified?
- Did operators respond concisely?
- Did ANCO have enough information for debrief?
