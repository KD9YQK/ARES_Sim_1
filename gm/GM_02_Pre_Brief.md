# GM Pre-Brief Guide

## Purpose

Use this document for the 30-minute pre-game briefing before the active radio simulation.

The purpose of the exercise is to train radio communication, net control, logging, tracking, prioritization, and information handling. It is not a rescue skills exercise and it is not a test of operational heroics.

## Suggested 30-Minute Pre-Brief Schedule

### 0:00–0:05 — Welcome and Exercise Purpose

Explain:

- This is a simulated emergency communications exercise.
- The active radio portion lasts one hour.
- The goal is to practice radio procedure, message clarity, directed-net operation, and situation tracking.
- Mistakes are expected and useful for debrief.

### 0:05–0:10 — Role Assignments

Assign:

- Game Master
- Net Control Operator
- Assistant Net Control Operator
- OP 1 Rescue Alpha
- OP 2 Rescue Bravo
- OP 3 Medical Staging
- OP 4 Utilities / Damage Assessment
- OP 5 Shelter / Resident Accountability
- OP 6 Logistics / Access

Explain that operators are communicators attached to fictitious teams, not commanders of those teams.

### 0:10–0:15 — Radio and Legal Reminders

Explain:

- Use real amateur callsigns as required.
- Tactical assignments may be used, but they do not replace callsign identification.
- Keep transmissions short and plain-language.
- This is an exercise. Do not transmit real false emergency information in a way that could be confused with actual traffic.
- If a real emergency occurs, use: “Real-world emergency traffic.”

### 0:15–0:20 — Scenario Boundary

Read the one-line scenario:

A severe storm caused partial collapse of an older apartment building, scattered utility hazards, blocked access routes, and conflicting reports about trapped residents.

Explain:

- Operators only know what is on their cards.
- Operators may not invent facts.
- Net Control does not see operator cards.
- The GM controls outcomes and inject responses.
- NCO directs the net and routes traffic.
- ANCO logs and tracks the incident picture.

### 0:20–0:25 — Operator Packet Review

Give each operator their card.

Operators may read their own card silently.

Do not allow operators to share card details with each other before the net.

Remind operators:

- Initial check-in scenes are reported during roll call.
- Timed events become available at the listed time.
- Conditional information is only provided if Net Control asks.
- Instructions from Net Control are relayed to the fictitious team leader.
- Wait for GM before reporting results of assigned actions.

### 0:25–0:30 — NCO/ANCO Setup

Confirm:

- Frequency or channel.
- Callsign and tactical naming plan.
- Net start time.
- Logging forms ready.
- Status tracker ready.
- GM and ANCO communication method.
- Timekeeper assigned.

NCO should prepare to open the net at simulation time 00:00.

## Safety Phrase

If a real emergency occurs, any participant may say:

> Real-world emergency traffic.

The simulation pauses immediately until resolved.

## GM Final Reminder

Tell participants:

“This exercise is about communication. Do not worry about finding the perfect answer. Communicate what you know, what you do not know, and what you need clarified.”
