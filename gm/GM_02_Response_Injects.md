# GM Response Injects

## Purpose

This document gives the Game Master conditional responses to operator reports and Net Control decisions.

The GM controls scenario reality. Operators and Net Control do not decide whether a task succeeds. If Net Control gives an instruction to a field team, the operator should relay it to the fictitious team leader. The GM then provides the result when appropriate.

Use these injects to reward good information gathering, expose missed details, and keep the one-hour exercise moving.

## Inject 1 — East Access Blocked

Approximate time: 00:10  
Trigger: Rescue Bravo reports arrival near east access.

Base situation:

Vehicle access is blocked by a fallen tree and debris. A narrow sidewalk path may be passable on foot.

If NCO asks whether vehicles can pass:

No. Vehicle access is blocked.

If NCO asks whether foot access is possible:

Foot access may be possible along the north sidewalk, but it is cluttered.

If NCO asks whether anyone is injured there:

No visible casualties from that position.

If NCO assigns the team to proceed on foot:

The operator should acknowledge that they are relaying the instruction to Bravo Team Leader. The GM may later allow Bravo to reach the east-side observation point.

If NCO does not ask follow-up questions:

Leave the access issue only partially understood. This may affect routing later.

## Inject 2 — Medical / Civilian Issue at South Entrance

Approximate time: 00:17 to 00:24  
Trigger: Rescue Alpha reports civilians, minor injuries, and medication/re-entry concern.

If NCO requests medical support:

Medical may send a small triage element if given a safe route and contact point.

If NCO asks for patient count:

Two visible minor injuries. One person is emotionally distressed and trying to retrieve medication.

If NCO asks whether there is a serious medical issue:

No confirmed serious injury at this time. The medication issue is unclear.

If NCO asks about the structure:

Rescue Alpha cannot confirm structural safety.

If NCO ignores the re-entry concern:

At 00:24, tell Rescue Alpha that the upset resident is now moving closer to the entrance and appears ready to go inside.

If NCO handles it well:

The resident remains outside while the team awaits further direction.

## Inject 3 — Downed Utility Line

Approximate time: 00:20  
Trigger: Utilities reports wire down near Pine and 6th.

If NCO asks whether energized:

Unknown. Treat as energized.

If NCO asks whether the area is safe:

No. The area should be avoided.

If NCO asks whether pedestrians are nearby:

No one is currently touching the wire, but pedestrians could enter the area.

If NCO asks for utility response ETA:

No ETA is available during the exercise window.

If NCO asks for perimeter or barricades:

Logistics may be able to support this later if assigned.

If NCO fails to notify all stations:

Allow this to become a routing hazard later, especially for Medical movement.

## Inject 4 — Route Recommendation

Approximate time: 00:24 or later  
Trigger: NCO asks Logistics for a safe route for Medical.

Possible Logistics response:

Avoid Pine and 6th. A possible route is west on 5th, then south on Cedar, approaching from the southwest.

GM note:

This is a recommendation, not a guarantee. NCO still needs to decide how to use it.

If NCO routes Medical through Pine and 6th:

Medical should hold or be rerouted because of the utility hazard.

If NCO routes Medical via the southwest route:

Medical can proceed if NCO provides a contact point.

## Inject 5 — Conflicting Missing-Resident Report

Approximate time: 00:30  
Trigger: Shelter reports possible missing resident in apartment 3B.

Base situation:

The report is unconfirmed and conflicting.

If NCO asks whether confirmed:

No. One resident says the person may still be inside. Another believes the person left earlier.

If NCO asks for apartment number:

Apartment 3B, second floor.

If NCO tasks Rescue Alpha or Bravo to verify:

The team may perform safe exterior observation or attempt exterior contact only. No entry is authorized by this inject.

If NCO treats it as confirmed trapped person:

Do not immediately correct them unless needed for safety. Note it for debrief as an information-handling issue.

If NCO ignores it entirely:

Keep it unresolved until the 00:55 Shelter update.

## Inject 6 — Medical Movement

Approximate time: 00:38  
Trigger: Medical Staging requests route, patient count, injury type, and contact point.

If NCO has provided safe route and contact point:

Medical can prepare to move toward the south parking lot contact point.

If NCO does not have a route:

Medical holds at staging and requests route clarification.

If NCO does not have patient count:

Medical asks for approximate number and injury type.

If Medical is sent to south parking lot via safe route:

At the final stabilization inject, Medical may be reported as reaching the contact point.

If Medical is routed through Pine and 6th:

Medical does not proceed. They request reroute due to the utility hazard.

## Inject 7 — Voices on East Side

Approximate time: 00:42 to 00:47  
Trigger: Rescue Bravo reports voices near east side.

Base situation:

Voices are heard from above or near the second floor. Damaged stairs are visible. Rescue Bravo cannot safely reach them.

If NCO asks how many:

After checking again, it appears to be two people.

If NCO asks where:

They appear to be on or near an east-side balcony.

If NCO asks whether injured:

No obvious injuries are visible, but medical status cannot be confirmed.

If NCO asks whether Rescue Bravo can reach them:

Not safely from their current position.

If NCO assumes they are the apartment 3B report:

Do not confirm that unless they ask. The balcony residents are not confirmed to be the 3B resident.

## Inject 8 — Logistics Resource Prioritization

Approximate time: 00:51  
Trigger: Logistics reports one crew and barricades available, asking for direction.

If NCO sends support to Pine and 6th:

Hazard perimeter improves. Utilities can report the area is better marked, but the wire is still unsafe.

If NCO sends support to south-side access:

Medical and resident movement improve near the south parking lot.

If NCO sends support to east blockage:

Work begins, but the blockage will not clear before the exercise ends.

If NCO tries to assign the crew to multiple tasks:

Logistics reports they cannot do all tasks at once with current resources.

If NCO does not assign the resource:

The crew remains staged and unused.

## Inject 9 — Shelter Accountability Update

Approximate time: 00:55  
Trigger: Shelter reports 17 residents and new information about apartment 3B.

Base situation:

A newly arrived resident says the apartment 3B occupant left with family before the collapse. Shelter cannot personally verify this.

If NCO asks whether the missing-person issue is resolved:

No. It is downgraded but not fully confirmed.

If NCO asks whether Rescue should stop all checks:

That is an operational decision controlled by GM/command. The better communications answer is to update the status as conflicting/unconfirmed.

If NCO notifies Rescue teams:

Rescue teams can acknowledge the update.

## Inject 10 — Final Stabilization

Approximate time: 00:57  
Trigger: Prepare for final roll call.

Choose the ending based on net performance.

Good coordination ending:

Incident is stabilizing. Medical reached the south contact point. Utility hazard is marked. Shelter accountability is improving. East-side residents are identified and awaiting technical rescue.

Mixed coordination ending:

Incident remains active. Medical movement was delayed. Utility hazard is known but not fully controlled. Shelter accountability is incomplete. East-side residents are identified but support is not fully coordinated.

Poor coordination ending:

Situation picture is fragmented. Several hazards are known but not routed clearly. Medical and logistics support were delayed or misdirected. The final roll call should reconstruct current status.

## GM Timing Guidance

Do not stack events too tightly if the net is still handling prior traffic.

If the net is busy, slide the next operator event by 1 to 3 minutes.

If the net is quiet, prompt the next timed operator event slightly early.

Do not solve problems for the players. Provide facts and consequences.
