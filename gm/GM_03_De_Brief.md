# GM De-Brief Guide

## Purpose

Use this guide for the 30-minute post-exercise debrief.

The debrief should focus on communications behavior, not blame. The goal is to identify what worked, what was unclear, and what can improve in future nets.

## Suggested 30-Minute Debrief Schedule

### 0:00–0:05 — Scenario Summary

GM gives a brief recap:

- Starting situation.
- Major events that occurred.
- How the situation ended.
- Whether the final incident picture was complete, partial, or fragmented.

### 0:05–0:10 — NCO Review

Discuss:

- Did NCO maintain a directed net?
- Were scheduled broadcasts made at 00:15 and 00:45?
- Was the 00:30 roll call and location update completed?
- Was the 00:59 final roll call completed?
- Did NCO prioritize traffic effectively?
- Did NCO ask useful clarifying questions?
- Did NCO distinguish confirmed, unconfirmed, and conflicting information?

### 0:10–0:15 — ANCO Review

Discuss:

- Were team locations tracked?
- Were hazards logged clearly?
- Were unresolved items visible?
- Did the status board reflect the current situation?
- Did ANCO capture changes over time?
- Could NCO use ANCO’s information during the net?

### 0:15–0:22 — Operator Review

Discuss:

- Did operators use callsign and tactical assignment?
- Did operators stay within their card information?
- Did operators avoid making command decisions?
- Did operators report uncertainty clearly?
- Did operators relay Net Control instructions back to team leaders?
- Did operators avoid inventing outcomes?

### 0:22–0:27 — Scenario-Specific Discussion

Discuss these incident items:

- East access blockage.
- Civilians near south entrance.
- Medication/re-entry concern.
- Pine and 6th downed utility line.
- Medical routing and contact point.
- Conflicting apartment 3B report.
- East-side voices/balcony residents.
- Limited logistics resource.
- Final shelter accountability update.

For each, ask:

- What was known?
- What was unknown?
- Was the information logged?
- Was it routed to the right station?
- Did the net overstate or understate the issue?

### 0:27–0:30 — Lessons Learned

Ask each participant for one short answer:

- One thing that worked well.
- One thing to improve next time.

GM closes with 2 to 3 practical takeaways.

## Evaluation Categories

Use these categories if scoring is desired.

### Net Control

- Directed-net control.
- Clear acknowledgments.
- Traffic prioritization.
- Clarifying questions.
- Scheduled net announcements.
- Location/status tracking.
- Final situation summary.

### ANCO

- Accurate logging.
- Tracking of teams and hazards.
- Clear unresolved-item list.
- Support to NCO.
- Debrief-quality notes.

### Operators

- Callsign use.
- Tactical assignment use.
- Concise reporting.
- Accurate interpretation of card events.
- Clear uncertainty language.
- No invented authority or outcomes.
- Proper response to Net Control instructions.

## Debrief Questions

Did the team maintain an accurate incident picture?

Were safety hazards broadcast to all stations?

Did operators report only what they knew?

Did NCO ask enough clarifying questions?

Did ANCO track what was confirmed versus unconfirmed?

Did any station make an operational decision outside their role?

Were resources prioritized appropriately?

Was the final roll call useful?

What should be changed before running this scenario again?
