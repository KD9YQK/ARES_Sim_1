# NCO / ANCO Instructions and Scripts

## Purpose

This document supports the Net Control Operator and Assistant Net Control Operator during the Urban Search and Rescue simulation.

The NCO controls the radio net. The ANCO supports by logging, tracking locations, maintaining the situation board, and communicating with the Game Master.

Operators are communicators assigned to fictitious field teams. They do not command those teams. Net Control may pass instructions to operators, who simulate relaying those instructions to their fictitious team leaders. The Game Master determines outcomes.

## NCO Responsibilities

- Open and close the directed net.
- Use required callsign identification.
- Conduct initial check-in.
- Maintain net discipline.
- Acknowledge traffic.
- Prioritize safety and priority traffic.
- Ask clarifying questions.
- Route information between stations.
- Request GM clarification through ANCO when needed.
- Conduct required status broadcasts at 00:15 and 00:45.
- Conduct roll call and location update at 00:30.
- Conduct final roll call at 00:59.

## ANCO Responsibilities

- Log all meaningful traffic.
- Track each team’s location and status.
- Track hazards and blocked routes.
- Track unresolved issues.
- Mark information as confirmed, unconfirmed, conflicting, or resolved.
- Maintain a situation board or map.
- Relay GM injects to NCO when appropriate.
- Prepare summary information for debrief.

## Directed Net Principles

All stations should call Net Control before passing traffic unless they have emergency simulation traffic.

NCO should avoid long transmissions.

NCO should ask for missing details when reports are incomplete.

NCO should not make up outcomes. If an outcome is needed, request it from the Game Master through ANCO.

## Required Net Start Script — 00:00

Use or adapt:

> This is **[CALLSIGN]**, Net Control, opening the Dupage County ARES Urban Search and Rescue Simulation Net. This is a directed net. All participating stations will check in with callsign, tactical assignment, current location, and immediate traffic if any. This is strictly an exercise. Real-world emergency traffic may break in at any time by stating “Real-world emergency traffic.”

Then conduct roll call:

> Rescue Alpha, check in.

> Rescue Bravo, check in.

> Medical Staging, check in.

> Utilities, check in.

> Shelter, check in.

> Logistics, check in.

After all check-ins:

> This is **[CALLSIGN]**, Net Control. All participating stations are checked in. This remains a directed simulation net. Stations hold routine traffic unless called. Priority simulation traffic may call Net Control.

## Required Simulation Net Status Broadcast — 00:15

Use or adapt:

> This is **[CALLSIGN]**, Net Control. This is the Dupage County ARES Urban Search and Rescue Simulation Net. This remains a directed net. Participating stations should hold routine traffic unless called. Priority or emergency simulation traffic may break at any time.  Real-world emergency traffic may break in at any time by stating “Real-world emergency traffic.”

Optional status summary:

> Current known status: [brief summary of known locations, hazards, and unresolved items].

Do not summarize information unless it has been reported on the net or provided by GM.

## Required Status Roll Call and Location Update — 00:30

Use or adapt:

> This is **[CALLSIGN]**, Net Control. This is the Dupage County ARES Urban Search and Rescue Simulation Net.  Conducting scheduled status roll call and location update. Participating stations respond with callsign, tactical assignment, current location, current status, and any routine or priority traffic only. 

Call each station in order.

> [CALLSIGN] Rescue Alpha, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Rescue Bravo, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Medical Staging, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Utilities, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Shelter, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Logistics, check in with location and status.

> Status roll call complete. Priority or emergency simulation traffic may break at any time.  Real-world emergency traffic may break in at any time by stating “Real-world emergency traffic.”

If a station breaks in with priority traffic, acknowledge it and handle it before continuing the roll call.

Example:

> Station with priority traffic, go ahead.

After handling the priority item:

> This is **[CALLSIGN]**, Net Control, resuming scheduled roll call. [Next station], check in with location and status.

## Required Simulation Net Status Broadcast — 00:45

Use or adapt:

> This is **[CALLSIGN]**, Net Control. This is the Dupage County ARES Urban Search and Rescue Simulation Net. Current known simulated incident status follows: [brief status summary]. This remains a directed net.  Priority or emergency simulation traffic may break at any time.  Real-world emergency traffic may break in at any time by stating “Real-world emergency traffic.”

The 00:45 summary should distinguish:

- Confirmed information.
- Unconfirmed information.
- Hazards.
- Blocked access.
- Unresolved requests.

Avoid overstating uncertainty. Use phrases like:

- “Reported but unconfirmed.”
- “Known hazard.”
- “No ETA available.”
- “Unable to confirm at this time.”

## Required Final Roll Call and Net Close — 00:59

Use or adapt:

> This is **[CALLSIGN]**, Net Control. Conducting final roll call for the Dupage County Urban Search and Rescue Simulation Net. Participating stations respond with callsign, tactical assignment, final location, current status, and any unresolved routine or priority traffic.

Call each station in order.

> [CALLSIGN] Rescue Alpha, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Rescue Bravo, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Medical Staging, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Utilities, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Shelter, check in with location and status.

> [CALLSIGN] Logistics, check in with location and status.

After final responses:

> This is **[CALLSIGN]**, Net Control. The Dupage County ARES Urban Search and Rescue Simulation Net is complete. All participating stations are to return to the EOC and prepare for debrief.  This repeater system is returned to regular amateur use.

## Common NCO Follow-Up Questions

For location reports:

- What is your exact location?
- Are you on scene or enroute?
- Is the route passable for vehicles, foot traffic, or both?

For hazards:

- Is anyone injured?
- Is the area safe or unsafe?
- Is the hazard confirmed or suspected?
- Does this affect other team routes?

For medical reports:

- Approximate number of patients?
- Type of injuries?
- Exact contact point?
- Is the area safe for Medical to approach?

For missing-person reports:

- Is this confirmed or unconfirmed?
- Who reported it?
- What location or apartment number?
- Is there conflicting information?

For resource requests:

- What resource is requested?
- Where is it needed?
- What is the priority?
- Are there constraints or hazards on the route?

## ANCO Quick Tracking Categories

Track at minimum:

- Team name.
- Callsign.
- Current location.
- Current status.
- Last report time.
- Open requests.
- Hazards affecting the team.
- Assigned actions pending GM result.
- Track unresolved incident items

## Working With the GM

When NCO needs an outcome, ANCO should present exact notes to the GM.  The GM may respond at that moment, in a period of time, or maybe even not at all, depending on the traffic.

Do not invent outcomes. Route outcome questions to GM.
