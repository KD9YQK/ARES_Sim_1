# General Operator Instructions

## Purpose

You are participating in an over-the-radio emergency communications simulation. Your job is to practice clear, disciplined radio communication under a directed net.

You are not acting as the commander of your simulated team. You are the radio operator assigned to a fictitious field team. Your fictitious team leader makes team-level decisions. The Game Master controls what your team can do, what it observes, and what happens as a result of actions requested through Net Control.

## Required Callsign Use

Use your real amateur radio callsign as required by amateur radio rules.

Use your tactical assignment in addition to your callsign.

Example:

> Net Control, this is **[CALLSIGN]**, Rescue Alpha with simulation priority traffic.

A tactical name does not replace required station identification.

Always include words like **Simulation** or **Exercise** to properly identify the traffic is for this event, and not real-world traffic.

## What You May Communicate

You may communicate:

- Your callsign and tactical assignment.
- Your simulated team location.
- Your team status.
- What your operator card says your team observes.
- What your fictitious team leader tells you to report.
- Requests your fictitious team leader asks you to relay.
- Clarifying information from your card when Net Control asks for it.
- Instructions received from Net Control back to your fictitious team leader.

## What You May Not Do

You may not:

- Assign your team to a task.
- Relocate your team unless your card, Net Control, or the Game Master provides that change.
- Enter buildings, clear hazards, treat patients, move civilians, direct traffic, or deploy resources unless the Game Master provides that result.
- Invent new resources, hazards, victims, road closures, injuries, agency actions, or outcomes.
- Declare a report confirmed unless your card or the Game Master says it is confirmed.
- Solve the scenario outside the radio net.

## Handling Instructions from Net Control

When Net Control gives your team an instruction, do not immediately invent the result.

A proper response is:

> Net Control, this is **[CALLSIGN]**, Rescue Alpha copies **INFO**. I will relay that to Alpha Team Leader.

Then wait for the Game Master to provide the result, either directly or through Net Control.

## Handling Unknowns

If Net Control asks for information that is not on your card, do not guess.

Use plain language:

- “Unable to confirm from our current position.”
- “Unknown at this time.”
- “I do not have that information.”
- “I can relay that question to the team leader.”

## Traffic Priority

Use good judgment when requesting airtime.

Routine traffic can wait until called.

Priority traffic includes safety hazards, blocked access, changing conditions, or information that affects other teams.

Emergency simulation traffic should be used only when your card clearly indicates an urgent life-safety matter.

## Exercise Phrase

All exercise traffic should be understood as simulated. If a real emergency occurs, use:

> Real-world emergency traffic.

That phrase overrides the simulation.

## Conduct

Keep transmissions brief. Speak clearly. Avoid long explanations. Give location, condition, request, and uncertainty where appropriate.

Your goal is not to be heroic. Your goal is to communicate accurately.
