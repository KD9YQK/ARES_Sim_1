# Operator Card 4 — Utilities / Damage Assessment Radio Operator

## Assignment

Tactical assignment: **Utilities**  
Fictitious team leader: **Utility Assessment Lead**  
Starting location: **Heading southbound on Maple St and 2nd St**  
Role: Radio operator for a field utility and damage assessment team

## Operator Rule

You are the communicator for Utilities. You do not command the utility team. You report what Utility Assessment Lead tells you, what your card says your team observes, and what Net Control asks you to relay.

## Initial Check-In Scene — 00:00

Your team is mobile heading southbound near Maple and 2nd St.

Your team is checking storm damage and possible utility hazards.

You have not yet confirmed any specific hazard.

Utility Assessment Lead tells you to check in and report that you are mobile in the area.

Communications task:

Check in with callsign, tactical assignment, location, and current assignment.

## Timed Event — 00:20

Your team finds a wire down across part of the street near Maple St and 5th St.

You observe:

- Wire down near standing water or puddles.
- You cannot confirm whether the wire is energized.
- No one is currently touching it.
- Pedestrians could enter the area if not warned.
- Utility Assessment Lead says it should be treated as unsafe.

Communications task:

Report the hazard to Net Control. Be clear that the wire status is unknown and should be treated as unsafe.

Do not claim the power is shut off. Do not declare the area safe.

## Conditional Information

Only provide these details if Net Control asks.

If asked whether the line is energized:

Unknown. Your team recommends treating it as energized.

If asked whether anyone is injured:

No injuries observed at this location.

If asked whether traffic can pass:

Recommend avoiding Maple St and 5th St.

If asked whether the utility company has been notified:

Utility Assessment Lead can request that notification through Net Control, but you cannot confirm outside utility response unless the Game Master provides it.

If asked whether your team can secure the area:

Your team can observe and report. Physical perimeter control would require direction and resources from Net Control/Game Master.

## Restrictions

Do not shut off utilities, clear wires, declare areas safe, or direct pedestrians unless the Game Master provides that result. You only communicate the hazard and team leader’s assessment.
